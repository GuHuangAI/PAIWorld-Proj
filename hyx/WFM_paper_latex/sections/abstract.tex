\begin{abstract}
World foundation models (WFMs) have emerged as powerful simulators for physical environments, yet existing approaches predominantly operate in a single-view setting and lack the multi-view 3D consistency essential for robotic manipulation. Robotic systems inherently rely on multiple cameras--egocentric, eye-to-hand, and wrist-mounted--to capture complementary viewpoints for policy learning, but current multi-view world models merely concatenate view tokens without explicit geometric reasoning, resulting in cross-view object drift, depth inconsistency, and texture misalignment that propagate errors to downstream planning and control. We identify two fundamental deficiencies in existing multi-view approaches: (1)~the lack of an explicit \emph{inter-view communication mechanism}, which forces each viewpoint to generate independently without coordinating with others, and (2)~the absence of a \emph{3D geometric prior}, which leaves the model without guidance on what constitutes physically correct cross-view structure. Addressing these two deficiencies requires both an information pathway across views and a geometric learning signal that steers this pathway--neither alone suffices, as communication without geometric guidance collapses to trivial solutions, while geometric priors without inter-view channels cannot propagate constraints across viewpoints. Based on this analysis, we present \methodname{}, a framework that augments diffusion-transformer-based world models with three components: \textbf{Geometric Rotary Position Embedding} encodes camera ray directions and extrinsic poses into the attention mechanism via rotary position encoding, \textbf{Cross-View Attention} blocks provide explicit inter-view communication channels, and \textbf{Latent 3D-REPA} distills 3D-aware features from 3D foundation models as the geometric learning signal. Built upon the Cosmos world foundation model, \methodname{} achieves state-of-the-art multi-view 3D consistency on robotic manipulation benchmarks and enables downstream applications including model-based planning, world action models, and multi-view policy distillation.
\end{abstract}
