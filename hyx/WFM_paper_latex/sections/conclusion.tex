\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented \methodname{}, a framework for achieving 3D-consistent multi-view generation in world foundation models for robotic manipulation. Our analysis shows that multi-view 3D consistency requires two conditions to be satisfied jointly: an inter-view communication mechanism and a 3D geometric prior. Cross-View Attention provides the communication channel for viewpoints to exchange information, Latent 3D-REPA supplies the geometric learning signal that guides this exchange toward physically correct 3D representations, and Geometric Rotary Position Embedding encodes camera ray directions and extrinsic poses into the attention mechanism as the common reference frame. We demonstrated that neither condition alone is sufficient: communication without geometric guidance leads to trivial shortcuts, while geometric priors without inter-view channels cannot enforce cross-view coherence. Their combination produces consistent multi-view 3D generation.

Built upon the Cosmos world foundation model~\cite{nvidia2025cosmos}, \methodname{} achieves state-of-the-art multi-view consistency on robotic manipulation benchmarks as measured by cross-view reconstruction metrics (DUSt3R/MASt3R), depth coherence, and optical flow consistency. The improved 3D consistency translates directly to downstream embodied tasks: model-based robotic planning benefits from physically plausible imagined trajectories, world action models exhibit stronger action-visual causal alignment, and multi-view policy distillation produces more effective manipulation policies.

Several promising directions remain for future work. First, extending \methodname{} with explicit \emph{action conditioning} would enable controllable multi-view imagination directly from robot action commands, strengthening its utility as a robotic simulator. Second, incorporating \emph{physical interaction modeling}, such as contact dynamics, deformable objects, and fluid simulation, would push 3D consistency beyond geometry into physics-aware world modeling. Third, scaling to \emph{long-horizon planning} scenarios will require maintaining 3D consistency over extended temporal rollouts, potentially through hierarchical or recurrent architectures. Finally, the modular design of our three components makes them applicable beyond Cosmos to any DiT-based world foundation model, opening the path toward a universal 3D-consistent world model backbone for embodied intelligence.
